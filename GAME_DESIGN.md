IPDF COMBAT CHRONICLE
Game Design Document v1.0

WORLD & TONE
An alien planet under siege. The terrain is geological and alive — strange formations, impossible colors, an atmosphere that feels wrong in the most compelling way. The sky shifts between deep purples, toxic ambers, and the cold black of near-space. The ground glows faintly where Voidheart Ore runs close to the surface.
The orc presence feels like an infection. Their structures are junk architecture — mismatched salvage bolted together without care, pipes and scaffolding and stolen technology humming with extracted energy. Nothing they build belongs here. Everything they build hurts the planet.
Tone: Grimdark arcade. Epic in scale, personal in motivation. Think Warhammer 40K meets classic arcade cabinet — overwhelming odds, individual heroism, and a universe that doesn't care if you survive.

THE RESOURCE — VOIDHEART ORE
A rare mineral found deep in this planet's crust. Glowing deep purplish-red with gold veins running through it. Visually unmistakable — players will recognize it immediately on mining platforms and in orc stockpiles.
The strategic threat: if fully extracted, Voidheart Ore can be refined into weapons capable of planetary-scale destruction. The IPDF's primary objective is to deny the orcs this capability. The deeper question — why this planet, why now — is the mystery that drives the campaign.

THE ENEMY — THE ORC COLLECTIVE
A biomechanical warrior race driven by survival and conquest. Their homeworld is stripped bare. They move through the galaxy consuming resource-rich planets, enslaving native populations as mining labor, and leaving nothing behind.
Visual identity: Green skin fused with brass and steel augmentations. Neon fur plumes mounted on helmets indicate rank — the larger the plume, the more dangerous and significant the target. Their ships and structures feel grown as much as built. Organic brutality meets industrial salvage.
Captive populations — human and otherwise — are forced into tan space suits branded with a red orc paw. Property marks. Your brother or sister is somewhere in that system.

THE PLAYER & PERSONAL MISSION
An IPDF fighter pilot. Name chosen by the player at game start.
Your sibling — name also chosen at game start — flew an IPDF-A7-042 "Dawn Breaker", a Generation 7 Strikewing assault frame. They went down on a mission and are presumed captured. Somewhere in the orc prison network they're wearing a tan suit with a red paw on it.
The war is the mission. Finding them is the reason.

COMMAND
General Carl "Bad Brad" Bradley delivers all mission briefings and strategic updates. He is the voice of the war — pragmatic, experienced, not unsympathetic to your personal mission but unwilling to let it compromise the objective. The tension between his orders and your search is a thread that runs through the entire campaign.

COMBAT ZONE STRUCTURE
The battlefield is a wide alien landscape. The player moves freely left and right across it. The world scrolls relative to player movement — if you move left, the ground shifts right. If you hold position, everything holds still. You are not racing through a corridor. You are hunting across a battlefield you control.
Primary objectives per mission: weapon emplacements and mining platforms. Destroy the operation. Deny them the ore. The mission ends when objectives are cleared or you are destroyed.

FLEET REGISTRY FORMAT
IPDF-[Class Code][Generation]-[Hull Number] "Call Sign"
Class codes: A = Assault (Strikewing) · C = Cruiser (Tempest) · H = Hulk (Hammerfall)
Generation = major production revision. Hull number = individual craft identifier. Call sign = pilot-earned, campaign-earned, or ceremonially assigned.
Example: IPDF-A7-042 "Dawn Breaker"

SHIP CLASSES
Strikewing — Assault Class (A)
Role: High-speed strike interceptor.
Philosophy: Survive through movement and precision.
The lightest combat frame. Extreme maneuverability, light shielding, limited bombardment. Rewards pilot skill and timing above all else. Your sibling's class. Dawn Breaker was a Strikewing.
Tempest — Cruiser Class (C)
Role: Multi-role warship and fleet backbone.
Philosophy: Survive through timing and versatility.
Balanced across mobility, shielding, and firepower. The only mid-tier ship capable of deploying Nightfall bombs. Reliable across all combat scenarios without excelling at any single extreme.
Hammerfall — Hulk Class (H)
Role: Orbital siege platform and area denial anchor.
Philosophy: Survive through overwhelming force.
Heavily armored, slow-moving, devastating. Expanded bomb capacity, massive shield reserves. Built to absorb punishment and deliver catastrophic bombardment. A fleet anchor, not a skirmisher.

WEAPON SYSTEMS
All IPDF vessels share a standardized weapons platform with class-based capacity differences.
PX-9 Rapid Plasma Array
Standard on all classes. Rapid-fire direct-aim energy weapon. No tracking. Lightest system. Primary sustained damage output. Rewards accuracy and manual targeting. Effective against light armor and exposed structural targets.
CM-3 Scatter Lance — Cluster Missiles
Standard on all classes. Single missile splits into three submunitions on approach. Submunitions scatter near impact point. Moderate area damage. Slower reload than plasma. Effective against clustered targets and fortified positions.
NF-1 "Nightfall" Gravitic Siege Bomb
Exclusive to Tempest and Hammerfall. High-mass bomb with gravity-assisted arc trajectory. Slow descent, large blast radius, high precision required. Load capacity: Tempest carries 3 per mission, Hammerfall carries 6 to 12 depending on hull configuration. High risk, high reward.

DEFENSIVE SYSTEMS
Universal — FLR-2 "Flair"
Standard on all classes. Emits a sensor distortion burst. Breaks all active tracking locks. Destroys or disrupts in-flight tracking weapons. Prevents new lock acquisition briefly. Does not affect unguided projectiles. Cooldown: 15 seconds. A reactive survival tool built for timing, not immunity.
Strikewing — OD-1 "Overdrive"
Temporarily overclocks engines and forces an evasive roll. Rapid forward acceleration and a brief evasion window. No invulnerability. Survival depends entirely on timing and pilot control. Cooldown: 60 seconds. The Strikewing survives by movement.
Tempest — PS-5 "Pulse Shield"
Deploys a luminous energy barrier beneath the hull. Deflects all incoming attacks — tracking and non-tracking — for 5 seconds. Full but temporary protection for bombing runs, retreats, or holding position. Cooldown: 60 seconds. The Tempest survives by timing.
Hammerfall — PF-1 "PlanetFall"
Final authorization siege purge. One use per mission. Manual confirmation required. Releases all remaining Nightfall bombs, launches all remaining cluster missiles, jettisons remaining fuel cells, and converts shield reserves into blast amplification. Creates a non-survivable blast zone beneath the full length of the vessel. After activation: all bombs depleted, all missiles depleted, fuel critically reduced. Ship remains operable but strategically crippled. PlanetFall is not a shield. It is catastrophic area denial. The Hammerfall survives by annihilation.

ENEMY DESIGN DIRECTION
Ground emplacements should feel like orc junk engineering — salvaged, brutal, mismatched. Pipes running the wrong direction, exposed mechanisms, structures that look like they were bolted together under pressure and left to rust. Energy weapons should feel corrupted rather than refined — the technology works but it shouldn't.
Rank indicators: neon fur plumes on all orc units. Small plume = basic emplacement operator. Large plume = high value target, commander, or elite installation. Players learn to read plume size as a threat and priority indicator.
Mining platforms show active Voidheart Ore extraction — the ore's purplish-red glow visible through pipes and collection chambers.

GUNNER MODE
The second playable perspective. You are an orc ground defense operator — or a captured slave forced to man a gun — shooting down incoming IPDF fighters. The battlefield from below. Every design decision made for Pilot Mode should inform Gunner Mode's mirror experience.
